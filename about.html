About WANG

Building digital access and rural innovation from Pakistan.

WANG (We Are New Generation) is a registered non-profit in Pakistan. Community-led work began in 2014 from Ahmed Abad Wang; the WALI innovation lab was established in 2021 to bridge the digital divide with technology-driven programs for communities that are usually left last.

Our story

The urgency came from access.

The education crisis during COVID-19 exposed how impossible a successful future becomes when digital literacy and reliable internet are missing in rural Pakistan.

COVID-19 made the divide visible

Thousands of students in Balochistan were locked out of online classes because they lacked both digital skills and reliable internet access. That gap is one of the reasons WANG exists.

Rural access is not just an education issue

The divide affects agriculture, livelihoods, social inclusion, and the ability of communities to participate in the digital world on fair terms.

Balochistan is the starting point

Building in Bela keeps the work close to real community conditions instead of designing from a distance and hoping it translates later.

Founded by Qaisar Roonjha — roots in 2014, WALI in 2021

WANG’s public story begins in 2014 with community programs and the first website. Today it operates as a registered non-profit; WALI (2021) is the innovation lab that delivers camps and field programs in Lasbela.

The WANG model

Non-profit, lab, products.

WANG's structure links institutional trust with local execution and national-scale products, ensuring that rural insights become public systems.

Non-profit (WANG)

WANG provides the mission root: public registration, donor legitimacy, field relationships, girls' access, and long-term community trust that only a non-profit institutional presence can create.

Lab (WALI)

WALI is the working lab: digital literacy camps, youth pathways, AI learning, enterprise support, and local experimentation happen close to the people they are meant to serve in Lasbela, Balochistan.

Products

The initiatives scale nationally: Urdu AI, PakSpeed, PakEducate, WIRE, and Darwaza turn rural insight into usable tools, public platforms, and systems that can travel far beyond Balochistan.

Registration and location

Institutional facts

Registered non-profit

WANG is a registered non-profit organization under Pakistan's Societies Registration Act, providing the legal and institutional foundation for all six initiatives.

Location

Ahmed Abad Wang, Bela, District Lasbela, Balochistan, Pakistan 90050. The WALI innovation lab operates from this location, serving rural communities across the district and beyond.

Governance

Registered institution, accountable delivery.

Legal structure

WANG operates as a registered non-profit under Pakistan’s Societies Registration Act. Statutory filings, banking, partnerships, and major institutional commitments are maintained in line with that framework.

Field accountability

Program execution is led by the founder together with WALI lab leadership, trainers, and initiative leads, with public documentation (media, platforms, and this site) as the primary transparency layer for communities and partners.
