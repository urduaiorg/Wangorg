Why Digital Literacy Matters

ڈیجیٹل خواندگی کیوں ضروری ہے؟

Every Pakistani Deserves Digital Access

Digital literacy is no longer optional. Government services are moving online. Banking is going digital. Education increasingly requires internet access. Healthcare information is available digitally. Job markets demand basic tech skills.

For rural communities in Balochistan and across Pakistan, the gap between those with digital skills and those without is growing. Children who learn basic computer skills have better education outcomes. Women with digital access have more economic opportunities. Farmers with smartphone skills can access better market prices.

WANG and WALI believe that digital literacy is a fundamental right, not a luxury. Through free, community-based training in local languages, the digital divide can be bridged one village at a time.

How WANG & WALI Bridge the Divide

ہم ڈیجیٹل تقسیم کو کیسے ختم کر رہے ہیں

1

Community Training Camps

Free multi-day digital literacy camps in rural villages. Teaching basic computer skills, internet safety, and smartphone use in Urdu and local languages.

2

Urdu AI Platform

Pakistan's largest free AI literacy platform reaching 800,000+ learners through WhatsApp, YouTube, and the web -- all content in Urdu for maximum accessibility.

3

WALI Class Program

Regular, structured training sessions building digital skills week by week. From basics to intermediate, with hands-on practice and community support.

4

WIRE Women's Program

Dedicated digital skills and enterprise training for rural women through the mother-daughter innovation model, ensuring no one is left behind.

5

PakSpeed Research

Community-owned internet speed testing and connectivity research to identify infrastructure gaps and advocate for better digital access in underserved areas.

6

Youth Mentorship

Guiding young people toward technology careers, digital entrepreneurship, and further education through mentorship and practical skill development.

ڈیجیٹل خواندگی کیا ہے؟

ڈیجیٹل خواندگی سے مراد کمپیوٹر، انٹرنیٹ، موبائل فون، اور دیگر ڈیجیٹل ٹولز کو مؤثر اور محفوظ طریقے سے استعمال کرنے کی صلاحیت ہے۔ اس میں آن لائن معلومات تلاش کرنا، ای میل بھیجنا، سوشل میڈیا استعمال کرنا، اور ڈیجیٹل حفاظت شامل ہے۔

Why does digital literacy matter in Pakistan?

Pakistan has over 220 million people, but digital literacy rates remain low, especially in rural areas. In Balochistan, internet penetration is among the lowest in the country. Digital literacy unlocks access to education, healthcare information, government services, and economic opportunities.

کیا پاکستان میں مفت کمپیوٹر ٹریننگ دستیاب ہے؟

جی ہاں، WANG اور WALI بلوچستان میں مکمل طور پر مفت ڈیجیٹل خواندگی پروگرام چلاتے ہیں۔ ان میں بنیادی کمپیوٹر مہارتیں، انٹرنیٹ کا استعمال، موبائل ایپس، اور ڈیجیٹل حفاظت شامل ہے۔ تمام تربیت اردو میں ہوتی ہے۔

How is WANG bridging the digital divide in Balochistan?

WANG bridges the digital divide through WALI's community-based programs including Digital Literacy Camps, WALI Class regular training, the Urdu AI platform (800,000+ learners), PakSpeed internet infrastructure research, and WIRE women's empowerment. These programs operate from Ahmed Abad, Lasbela.

بلوچستان میں انٹرنیٹ کی صورتحال کیسی ہے؟

بلوچستان پاکستان کا سب سے بڑا صوبہ ہے لیکن انٹرنیٹ کی رسائی میں سب سے پیچھے ہے۔ بہت سے دیہی علاقوں میں 3G/4G سروس محدود ہے، براڈبینڈ کنکشن نایاب ہیں، اور ڈیجیٹل خواندگی کی شرح بہت کم ہے۔

How can I start learning digital skills for free with WANG?

Visit walipak.com/digital-literacy-pakistan.html to learn about WANG's available programs delivered through WALI. You can also join the WALI WhatsApp community for updates on upcoming training camps and online resources. All WANG programs are free and taught in Urdu.
