# Site Hierarchy Recommendation

## Source of truth

This hierarchy is aligned to:
- `WANG_Organizational_Profile_2026.docx`
- WANG's legal and institutional status as the registered nonprofit
- the distinction between nonprofit institution, flagship lab, flagship platform, core programs, and secondary ecosystem products

## Core structure

- `WANG` = the nonprofit parent institution
- `WALI` = WANG's rural innovation lab
- `Urdu AI` = flagship national AI education platform
- `WIRE` = women-led social enterprise operating under WANG
- `ADI` = girls and adolescent leadership program
- `Pakistan Scottish Scholarship`, `Las Homes`, `Climate Action & Resilience` = signature nonprofit programs
- `PakSpeed` = supporting ecosystem evidence platform
- `PakEducate`, `Darwaza` = secondary ecosystem products unless stronger public proof is approved

## Website architecture rule

The website should feel like:
1. a nonprofit authority site first
2. a rural innovation ecosystem second
3. a product portfolio third

## Copy rule

Use wording like:
- `WANG is a registered nonprofit organization based in Lasbela, Balochistan.`
- `WALI is WANG's rural innovation lab.`
- `Urdu AI is a flagship AI education initiative built through the WANG ecosystem.`
- `WIRE is a women-led social enterprise operating under WANG.`
- `ADI is WANG's adolescent girls and life-skills program.`

Avoid wording that makes all sub-brands sound like equal standalone institutions.

## Footer rule

Preferred footer ecosystem links:
- WALI
- Urdu AI
- WIRE
- PakSpeed

Do not foreground `PakEducate` or `Darwaza` in the main footer unless leadership explicitly wants them promoted and can support that decision with stronger institutional proof.
