Location authority

Rural innovation rooted in Lasbela — not parachuted in from a distant HQ.

WANG’s institutional identity is tied to Ahmed Abad Wang, Bela, District Lasbela, Balochistan: a coastal district where connectivity, schools, and climate shocks shape daily life. This page explains where we work, why it matters for digital equity, and how donors and partners can visit or co-design programs with our field team.

Where is Lasbela?

Connectivity and distance still define who can learn online.

National statistics still show Balochistan among Pakistan’s least connected provinces. In practice, many rural households lack reliable broadband — a constraint national “online-only” skills programs rarely solve without physical hubs and local facilitation.

Lasbela sits along the Arabian Sea; its towns and villages mix fishing economies, informal trade routes, and long travel distances to provincial services. When floods or heat waves land, roads and power are often first to fail — which is why WANG pairs digital programs with solar-aware lab operations, offline-first practice, and community-led recovery. For a deeper climate lens, read our Lasbela Rising journal piece and the climate resilience pillar page.

Presence

Fourteen years of community programs (2012–2026).

WANG’s public roots trace to 2012 community organizing and civic education in Ahmed Abad Wang — before the national visibility of Urdu AI or the opening of the WALI (WANG Lab of Innovation) physical hub in 2021. That sequence matters: the organization is not an imported franchise. Field relationships, local safety norms, and education pathways were built over years before the lab became a regional reference point for rural digital skills. The full institutional arc appears on About and Impact.

What we do here

WALI, scholarships, women’s pathways, relief, and climate work in one geography.

Lasbela is where WANG stress-tests ideas that later scale nationally (Urdu AI Dost networks, PakSpeed evidence, etc.). The lab is both classroom and credibility engine.

WALI lab & digital skills

Solar-supported lab time, rural cohorts, and facilitator-led practice — the subject of our digital literacy pillar. See also walipak.com for the lab brand.

Scholarships & school pathways

Lasbela-focused scholarship and re-enrollment work are part of WANG’s education continuity story — including community outreach that brings children back into formal schooling where families had disengaged after shocks or poverty cycles.

WIRE — women & girls

Mother–daughter and enterprise pathways build digital confidence without abstract jargon. The narrative fits our women & girls in technology pillar and Girl Rising — Lasbela journal story.

Flood relief & resilient housing

When Lasbela floods, WANG’s response blends rapid aid with longer recovery — documented in flood relief impact and community guidance posts from prior cycles.

Visit or partner

Field visits, audits, and co-design start with a direct conversation.

Journalists, funders, and CSR teams often begin with email and a short briefing call, then schedule a supervised field day from Bela. We prioritize child safety, dignity photography consent, and realistic timelines during humanitarian windows. Use Contact with “Lasbela field visit” in the subject line; share institution, proposed dates, and any donor due-diligence templates early.
