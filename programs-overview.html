Programs Delivered Through WALI

Seven practical routes from first access to long-term opportunity.

WANG's community programs are delivered through WALI, the rural innovation lab in Ahmed Abad Wang. Each program is designed around real local constraints: low digital access, limited training infrastructure, gender barriers, and the need for pathways that actually convert into confidence, income, or further learning.

Program Model

Not one-off workshops. A local pipeline.

The programs start with access and trust, then move into structured learning, mentoring, enterprise support, and real-world problem solving. That is what makes WANG's work durable instead of purely event-based.

01 • Core access

Digital Literacy Camps

Beginner-friendly camps introducing computer use, internet basics, safe digital behavior, typing, email, productivity tools, and device confidence for first-time learners in Lasbela.

Ad Grant Page

02 • Structured learning

WALI Class

A more sustained training environment for youth who need consistency, curriculum, and mentoring rather than drop-in exposure. This is where digital literacy becomes habit and practical competence.

Visit WALI

03 • Youth pathways

Youth Support Pathways

Guidance for young people moving from school into further study, training, public service, or community leadership. The emphasis is practical progression, not vague inspiration.

04 • Agriculture

WALI Zamindar

Agriculture-linked learning, climate awareness, and local experimentation for communities whose livelihoods depend on land, weather, and resource resilience.

05 • Enterprise

Business Development Pathways

Support for women, youth, and community members building early-stage ideas, livelihood opportunities, or micro-enterprises with local relevance and digital market potential.

06 • Institutional support

Consulting & Training

Program design, field training, digital literacy strategy, and AI education support for partners who want credible implementation in underserved communities.

07 • Evidence

Research & Data Collection

Community-grounded research and field documentation that turn local conditions into usable evidence for partners, donors, campaigns, and public-interest platforms like PakSpeed and Urdu AI.

How WANG Uses These Programs

Programs feed the wider ecosystem.

The programs delivered through WALI are not disconnected activities. They create the conditions for WANG's wider initiative portfolio to work: learners move into Urdu AI, field insights inform PakSpeed and research, and women's participation strengthens WIRE and enterprise pathways.

  • Community training creates the trust needed for long-term adoption.
  • Local delivery gives national products real context.
  • Every cohort becomes new proof for donors, partners, and media.

From camps to Urdu AI

First-time learners can continue learning in Urdu after in-person training ends.

From local needs to public platforms

Problems seen in the field become products, campaigns, and research assets.

From village proof to national credibility

Programs in one place create authority that scales across Pakistan.
